Celestial Navigation

Overview

Learning the night sky around you is like moving to a new city and trying to get a lay of the land. There are landmarks, highways, and unusual neighbors to discover for yourself.

Why Maps?

Find a book of celestial maps to help orient yourself when you go out to observe. By holding your hand out at arm's length and making a fist, you can roughly measure out 10 degrees in the sky. If you know your latitude, go up that many degrees from the horizon while facing due north, and you'll locate Polaris, the North Star.
